The Space

A Room That Smells of Salt and Blossom

The dining room at Sea Fruits seats thirty-two, across bare oak tables lit by beeswax candles in repurposed Lobstrawberry shells. The walls are lined with tide-washed driftwood and botanical prints sourced from a Victorian natural history archive — each one depicting a creature scientists could never quite explain.

We do not play music at volume. What you hear is the restaurant itself: the rhythm of the kitchen, the sea wind from the harbour, and the quiet pleasure of good conversation over impossible food.
