The Menu

Each dish is composed around a single hybrid specimen, prepared with techniques that honour both its aquatic and botanical nature.

First of the Sea & Vine

Small plates to open the palate

Lobstrawberry Bisque

$24

Slow-simmered shell stock reduced with roasted Lobstrawberry claw meat, finished with a float of strawberry cream and micro chervil. The bisque carries the tidal salinity of the claw alongside a gentle berry sweetness that blooms only at the end.

SignatureGluten-free

Tunana Sashimi, Black Sesame & Yuzu

$32

Hand-cut Tunana loin, aged for twelve hours, served across a smear of black sesame paste. A yuzu and banana-blossom vinaigrette provides acid brightness without masking the fish's innate creaminess. Four pieces, each a study in restraint.

Salmberry Crudo, Citrus Oil & Sea Salt

$28

Paper-thin Salmberry slices cured for twenty minutes in lemon zest and fleur de sel, dressed with cold-pressed citrus oil and a dot of fermented raspberry. Topped with a single edible flower from our tidal orchard.

Perchimmon Tartare

$26

Finely diced Perchimmon — a subtle river perch fused with the autumnal richness of ripe persimmon — mixed with shallot, capers, and a touch of aged sherry vinegar. Served with house-made rye crisps and a quail egg yolk.

Raw

Seabbage Salad, Fermented Shellfish & Dill

$22

Shredded Seabbage — a uni-kissed sea cabbage with the crunch of crucifer and the umami depth of sea urchin — dressed in a light vinaigrette with fermented cockle broth, crispy breadcrumbs, and fresh dill. A study in green and white, mineral and bright.

From the Deep Orchard

Principal courses, composed to satisfy

Whole Roasted Lobstrawberry

$68

A full Lobstrawberry, split and roasted over charcoal, basted in cultured butter and berry reduction every four minutes. Accompanied by charred spring onion, a small pot of drawn strawberry butter, and heirloom potato gratin. The defining dish of Sea Fruits.

Chef's ChoiceSignatureFor two

Tunana Collar, Miso & Plantain

$48

The prized collar of a mature Tunana — the richest cut — lacquered in white miso and twice-cooked under the grill. Served beside caramelised plantain, a bonito-banana dashi, and wilted sea purslane. Complex, generous, irreplaceable.

Signature

Salmberry en Papillote, Fennel & Grape

$44

A whole side of Salmberry sealed in parchment with shaved fennel, halved Concord grapes, white wine, and cold butter. Steamed in its own fragrant cloud, opened tableside. The steam alone is part of the experience.

Gluten-free

Codrange Stew, Saffron & Clementine

$42

Chunked Codrange — a flaky white fish with the faint citrus perfume of blood orange — simmered in a saffron-clementine broth with white beans, smoked paprika, and toasted sourdough rubbed with garlic. A dish of honest depth and quiet surprise.

Contains gluten

Prawnapple, Burnt Butter & Black Garlic

$46

A large Prawnapple — sweet pink prawn fused with the tropical brightness of pineapple — pan-seared until caramelised, finished in brown butter with black garlic, toasted coconut, and a squeeze of lime. Accompanied by cauliflower purée and micro coriander.

Gluten-free

Musselonberry Braise, White Wine & Thyme

$38

A pot of Musselonberry — the bivalve sweetness of mussel married with the honeyed stone-fruit notes of melon — steamed in white wine with shallot, fresh thyme, and a whisper of saffron. Served with crusty bread for soaking the fragrant broth. A humble classic reborn.

Sweet Tides

Desserts that close the loop between sea and sweetness

Lobstrawberry Shell Brûlée

$16

Classic crème brûlée infused with the natural brine-berry juices extracted from a Lobstrawberry shell, served inside a cleaned half-shell. The burnt sugar crust cracks to reveal a custard that tastes unmistakably of summer shoreline.

$14

A light, airy mousse made from the naturally sweet oils of the Tunana, set with agar and folded with whipped cream. Pooled in slow-cooked banana caramel, topped with a fragment of chocolate coral. Surprisingly easy to love.

Gluten-free

$15

Crisp meringue shell filled with whipped cream infused with sea-salt caramel. Topped with fresh seaberries and a dusting of edible coral salt. Tart, sweet, and briny all at once — a dessert that acknowledges the tidal orchard.
